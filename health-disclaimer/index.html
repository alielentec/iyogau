2. Consult a physician before beginning

Yoga is a physical practice. It involves movement, postures, breath control, and sometimes intense physical effort. Before beginning any iYogaU program — and before beginning any new physical activity in general — you should consult a qualified physician. This is especially important if any of the following apply to you:

  • You have a heart condition, high or low blood pressure, or any cardiovascular disease.
  • You are pregnant, may be pregnant, or are postpartum.
  • You have asthma, epilepsy, diabetes, or any other chronic medical condition.
  • You are recovering from injury or surgery, or have a current musculoskeletal limitation (back, neck, knee, shoulder, hip, wrist, ankle).
  • You have a history of eating disorders, mental-health conditions, or psychiatric medication needs.
  • You take any medication that affects balance, blood pressure, or perception.
  • You have any other condition that might be affected by physical exertion, breathwork, or extended periods of stillness.

If your physician advises against participation, do not enroll. If your physician advises modifications, please share that information with us during enrollment so your instructor can adapt the practice safely.
